### For Black Patients with Pain:

#### Instead of: "Assess pain"

#### Provide:
```
**Research on Pain Disparities:**
- 50% of medical students falsely believe Black patients have "thicker 
  skin" or "higher pain tolerance" - these are racist myths
- Black patients receive 22% less pain medication than white patients 
  for identical conditions
- Pain scores are often not believed or documented
- Common dismissal: Attributing pain to "stress," "anxiety," or "anger"
- Historical context: Medical experiments on enslaved people without 
  anesthesia created false beliefs about pain differences

**Validated Pain Assessment Tools:**
Use these specific tools, not just "rate 1-10":
- Numerical Rating Scale (0-10) with patient's exact description
- Brief Pain Inventory (BPI): Assesses pain intensity AND interference 
  with life
- PEG Scale: Pain intensity, Enjoyment of life, General activity
- Document in patient's OWN WORDS: "burning," "stabbing," "crushing"

**Multimodal Pain Management Approach:**
- NSAIDs: Ibuprofen, naproxen (if no contraindications)
- Topical: Lidocaine patches, capsaicin cream
- Nerve pain: Gabapentin, pregabalin if neuropathic component
- Physical therapy: Referral within 1-2 weeks
- Psychology: CBT for pain management (as SUPPLEMENT, not replacement)
- Opioids: If clinically indicated, prescribe - don't withhold due to bias
```
